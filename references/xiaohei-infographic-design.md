# Xiaohei Infographic Design (hybrid)

## Visual DNA

Pure white, black hand-drawn line work, large quiet areas, compact Chinese text, sparse red/orange/blue handwritten annotations, and one deadpan black Xiaohei doing each section's conceptual labor.

The result should feel like a sharp visual summary sketched on a whiteboard by a product-minded person — **not** a marketing poster, a PPT deck, or a card-grid dashboard.

This skill is **hybrid**: HTML owns all text; the per-section Xiaohei scenes are generated by `image_gen` (text-free, pure-white background) and composited into the page. See `image-gen-prompt.md` for the generation side; this file covers the page/layout side.

## Page Rules (default = single-page frameless flow)

- Width: **`1080px`**; screenshot at **`3x`** → ~3240px wide PNG. (The old 750px single-column build shrinks to an illegible strip on phones — do not use it.)
- Height: content-driven; no fixed page height.
- Background: `#fffefb` or `#ffffff`. No cream, gradient, paper texture, shadow, or noise — the generated scenes are white-bg, so any page tint would show a seam.
- Outer padding: ~`44px` top / `40px` bottom, `72px` sides.
- Vertical sequence: header → thesis → sections → takeaway → footer.
- **Sections are unframed rows**, not cards: illustration on one side, text on the other, separated by white space. Alternate the illustration side each section (`.flip`). **No borders around sections** — bordered card grids are the #1 way this drifts into "PPT".
- Keep at least 30% visible white space across the image.

### Mobile legibility & aspect

- Lay 3–5 sections in the flow. Past 5, the page gets too tall — trim, merge, or split into a second image.
- Target aspect ratio **1:1.4 – 1:1.9** (height/width). Verify after rendering:
  `python3 -c "from PIL import Image;w,h=Image.open('out.png').size;print(f'{w}x{h} 1:{h/w:.2f}')"`
- If ratio > 1.9: trim a section or tighten vertical gaps. Never fix tallness by shrinking width.
- Font sizes are sized for phone reading after platform downscaling (see Typography) — do not shrink them below the floor.

## Compositing the generated scenes

- Each scene goes in a `.shot` slot at ~38% width, `aspect-ratio: 3/2`, `object-fit: contain` on a white background → any generated image drops in seamlessly, no cropping artifacts, no visible box.
- Because the scene background is pure white and the page is pure white, **do not** add a border, shadow, or tinted panel around `.shot` — that would re-introduce the card look and a visible seam.
- Handwritten annotations are **HTML `.note` overlays** absolutely positioned at the scene's edges — they are crisp, correct, and editable. Do **not** bake annotation text into the generated image.

## Color

- Ink: `#171717` for line art, headings, Xiaohei.
- Body text: `#333333`.
- Muted text: `#76716b`.
- Paper line: `#e8e2d8`.
- Orange `#e8752e`: main flow / path / arrows / the title accent fragment.
- Red `#d83a2e`: warnings, friction, final results.
- Blue `#2f73d9`: secondary notes, system/AI/tool hints.
- Dark takeaway: `#171717` background with text `#f8f3ea`, gold `#ffbf58` emphasis.

Use one dominant accent per section. Do not distribute colors evenly just to decorate.

## Typography

```css
font-family: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "PingFang SC", "Noto Sans SC", "Helvetica Neue", Arial, sans-serif;
```

Annotations may use a handwriting stack: `"Xingkai SC", "Kaiti SC", "STKaiti", cursive`.

Scale (at 1080px, before 3x — sized so it stays legible after Telegram/WeChat downscaling):

- Main title: `48–52px`, weight `900`, line-height `1.14`.
- Subtitle/source: `20–22px`, weight `500`.
- Thesis: `28–32px`, weight `800`, line-height `1.5`.
- Section title: `32–36px`, weight `900`.
- Body points: `21–23px`, line-height `1.5`.
- Annotation note: `20–24px`, weight `700`.
- Takeaway: `25–27px`, weight `700`.

Keep Chinese labels short: section points 1 short line each; annotations 2–8 characters.

## Xiaohei

A small solid-black absurd worker: uneven black body, white dot eyes, thin legs, blank serious expression. Not cute, not a mascot. Must perform the section's core action (pull, push, weigh, stitch, sort, block, repair, catch, feed, record), embedded in the metaphor, not standing in the margin.

Keep identity consistent across all sections (same body/eyes/legs description in every prompt).

**Remove-Xiaohei test:** if deleting Xiaohei leaves the section fully intact, the metaphor is decorative — redesign it.

## Section Pattern

Each section row = one generated Xiaohei scene + a short title + 1–3 concise points + 1–2 annotation overlays.

Good section metaphors (invent a fresh one per article — do not reuse):

- "filtering noisy inputs" → Xiaohei stuck inside a funnel, catching only two notes.
- "strategy chooses constraints" → Xiaohei weighing two blocks on a scale.
- "workflow handoff" → Xiaohei stitching two boxes with an orange thread.
- "bad metric" → Xiaohei guarding a gate while red scraps pile up outside.
- "iteration loop" → Xiaohei turning a crank on a small loop machine.

## Avoid

- Bordered card grids / dashboards (the PPT trap).
- Polished vector illustration, formal architecture diagrams, dense node-link maps.
- Hero gradients, decorative orbs, bokeh, neon, glassmorphism.
- Emoji icons, big rounded pills full of text, large blocks of body copy.
- Beige/textured/tinted page background (shows a seam against white scenes).
- Baked-in text inside the generated scenes.
- Xiaohei as a corner sticker.

## QA

Pass criteria (full gate in `qa-gate.md`):

- The thesis is readable in one glance.
- Each section has one clear idea and one weird physical action by Xiaohei.
- The page reads as a frameless whiteboard flow, not a card grid.
- Background is seamless white; no panels/seams around scenes.
- Aspect ratio in 1:1.4–1:1.9; legible at phone size.
- Annotations sparse, short, and HTML-crisp; no text inside generated scenes.

Regenerate or edit if the first impression is "template infographic / PPT" rather than "strange clear whiteboard sketch."
